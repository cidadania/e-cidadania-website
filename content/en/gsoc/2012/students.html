When do I apply?

You can apply from March 26th to April 6th.

Where do I apply?

You will have to apply from the Google Summer of Code site.

What ideas can I apply for?

You can apply for any ideas in the ideas page or submit your own ideas. That would be great!

What information do you need in the application?

  • Full name
  • Country (or Timezone)
  • E-Mail address
  • iRC nickname (freenode.org)
  • GitHub/Gitorious repository
  • Will you be full time on the Google Summer of Code
  • Do you have other obligations that prevent you from working or take time away?
  • How many hours per week will you work on the GSoC?

I still have questions

I you have any questions you can contact with us through IRC and mailing lists (see resources) or directly sending an email to Oscar Carballal.

Are there any other requirements?

It would be nice if you submit a code snioppet or contribution to our repository in GitHub related with an issue of the bugtracker. It doesn't need to be a hard task, just pick one of the easy tasks, or make your own submission so we know that you have some of the technical knowledge we require.

Resources for students

Mailing lists

We strongly recommend the GSoC students to use the development list, but since the mentor (this year) speaks all of the three official e-cidadaia languages (english, spanish and galician) we will allow the student to choose the one that best fits him.

  • ecidadania-dev@freelists.org (Official development list (english))
  • ecidadania-users@freelists.org (Official users list (english))
  • ecidadania-es@freelists.org (Official spanish user list)
  • ecidadania-gl@freelists.org (Official galician user list)

To subscribe to a list you just have to send an email to -request@freelists.org with the subject "subscribe" and follow the instructions.
